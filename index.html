About Me

My name is Jack, I am an American-French ex-international economic lawyer and current entrepreneur. The transition from one field to the other happened at the end of my research program. I had to choose between pursuing my PhD thesis about Corporate Social Responsibility in International Investment Law and starting a company. I decided to found Manage My Power (MMP) with my associates and haven't regretted it so far. For various reasons, I recently went back to France, I am now working part-time and remotely for MMP while my associates are doing the ground work. I spent the other half of my time helping an Australian blockchain company to set up an office in Europe.

Start-up Experience

We created Manage My Power (MMP) with the idea to solve the problem that too many solar owners were facing: lack of returns. We then extended the scope of our product and it is now designed to guide businesses in their energy investments, and save them energy with what they already have. My role was initially to identify leads, negotiate and write contracts and design custom solutions for larger projects.

It wasn't easy to bring money in to continue R&D and pay ourselves. So, I started another venture doing some IT consulting for SMEs looking to automate their business processes. Even if it was successful on a financial level, my associate and I had to part ways. Now, MMP is mostly a B2B operation, we rely on energy efficiency specialists to sell our solution. My remote role is currently to identify leads, build business profiles, overview operations and perform some IT work.

Non-Profit Experience

Humanitarian work has been my calling since I was in middle school. I got my hands dirty right after high school and worked for an organization in South Africa and Ghana. A couple years later I achieved more substantial work in Uganda, a country which I now cherish and love. I worked every single minute I was awake, running a foster home/school from top to bottom. It was one of my biggest challenge and I handled it with strong organisational and negotiation skills. I had a firsthand experience with corruption issues, I was highly disappointed and I told myself I would only go back when I will be able to implement more efficient and trackable systems. And I believe that in a few years with money and blockchains we can develop those systems.

What I learned: I can't always be the nice guy and I must make extremely tough decisions for the greater good; a 70 hours work week is an easy week; sometimes you have to know when to say stop, breathe some fresh air, see friends and get back to it stronger.

Coding

My passion for computers started when I was young. I remember going to one of my parent's friends place and spend hours just watching him play around his screen. I wanted to learn how to code but we didn't have a computer and no one around me really knew what coding was about; people just told me that it was for smart people good in math. Thus, I slowly left that dream behind. About a year ago, I remembered that old dream. As soon as I started to get into it, I got obsessed.

Coding was so much more tangible, logical and challenging than law. I especially loved that results can be tested and confronted to objective standards (it was only later that I learned that coding also has a great part of subjectivity too). I first learned R to do data analysis because I thought it would benefit my business the most, then started to do some C because I wanted to understand how computers work under the hood. Finally, I learned JavaScript and started to code creatively. I did so by watching a YouTube channel called Coding Rainbow, I joined its community, and learned more there that I could ever dream of. Then came Blockchain.
